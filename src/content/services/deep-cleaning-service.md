## The reset your home has been asking for.

A deep clean is what it sounds like — we go deeper, slower, and into places a regular clean doesn't reach. Behind the toilet. Inside the oven. The grout you've been pretending not to notice. The baseboards, the ceiling fan blades, the tops of door frames.

Deep cleans are usually the right call in three situations: you're starting a recurring service and want to begin from zero, you haven't had the house professionally cleaned in a while, or you just want a seasonal reset.

## What we actually do differently

Everything in our standard cleaning is included. But deep cleans add hours of detail work that most services either skip or rush through:

- **Kitchen:** Inside of oven, inside of microwave, inside of fridge (if requested), cabinet fronts, range hood, and backsplash. Full degrease on stovetop and burner grates.
- **Bathrooms:** Grout scrubbing, shower door and track detail, buildup removal on fixtures and drains, baseboards, vents.
- **Floors:** Edges, corners, and under furniture where vacuums typically can't reach. Hand-scrub on tile and stone grout lines.
- **Surfaces:** Baseboards throughout the home, door frames, window sills, light switches and plates, vent covers, ceiling fan blades.
- **Detail work:** Cobweb removal in high corners, dusting of blinds and window treatments, wiping down interior doors.

## How long it takes

Deep cleans take two to four times longer than a standard cleaning, depending on home size and condition. A 2,000-square-foot home typically runs four to eight hours with a two-person team.

We'll give you a specific estimate after we've seen (or talked through) your space.

## Pricing

Deep cleaning is priced hourly or flat, depending on your preference. We'll walk through your home and send you a fixed quote you can count on — no surprise add-ons at the end.
